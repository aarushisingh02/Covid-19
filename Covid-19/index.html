How Virus Spread

Understanding the Spread

The coronavirus can spread in different ways. Understanding these helps us stay safe and take the right precautions.

  • Human Contact

    The virus can spread through direct contact such as handshakes, hugs, or touching an infected person.

  • Air Transmission

    When an infected person coughs, sneezes, or even talks, tiny droplets carrying the virus can travel through the air and infect others nearby.

  • Contaminated Objects

    The virus can survive on surfaces like door handles, phones, or tables. Touching these objects and then touching your face can lead to infection.

Corona Virus Symptoms

While symptoms of coronavirus may vary from person to person, some warning signs are more frequent than others. Being aware of these can guide you to seek medical care at the right time

  • Coughing And Sneezing

    Frequent coughing and sneezing are common symptoms that may indicate a respiratory infection, including COVID-19.

  • Hot Fever

    A persistent high temperature is one of the most common signs of coronavirus infection.

  • Strong Headacke

    Many patients experience strong or continuous headaches during the infection.

  • Shortness Of Breath

    Difficulty breathing or feeling breathless can be a serious symptom and requires medical attention.

  • Confusion

    In some cases, COVID-19 may cause dizziness, confusion, or difficulty concentrating.

  • Sore Throat

    A painful or irritated throat, especially when swallowing, is another early symptom.

Prevention Coronavirus

Taking simple precautions can greatly reduce the risk of coronavirus infection. By following healthy practices and maintaining hygiene, we can protect ourselves and those around us.

  • Wash Your Hands For 20sec

    Clean your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, or use a hand sanitizer with 60% alcohol.

  • Cover Nose and Mouth When Sneezing

    Always use a tissue or your elbow to cover coughs and sneezes, and dispose of tissues properly.

  • Wear A Mask If Available

    Wearing a mask in public places helps reduce the spread of respiratory droplets and keeps others safe.
